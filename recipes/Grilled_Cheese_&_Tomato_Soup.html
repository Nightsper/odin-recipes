Grilled Cheese & Tomato Soup

Description

A classic comfort food pairing, grilled cheese and tomato soup is the perfect balance of crispy, gooey, and savory flavors. The sandwich features golden-brown, buttery bread with melted cheese inside, creating a rich and satisfying bite. Whether made with cheddar, American, or a blend of cheeses, the combination is irresistibly delicious.

Served alongside a warm bowl of creamy tomato soup, this duo is both nostalgic and deeply comforting. The soup’s smooth texture and tangy-sweet tomato flavor complement the crispiness of the grilled cheese, making every bite and dip a perfect match. Whether enjoyed on a chilly day or as a quick and hearty meal, this timeless pairing never fails to satisfy.

Ingredients

For the Grilled Cheese:

  • 2 slices sourdough bread
  • 4 tbsp mayonnaise, divided
  • 2 tbsp butter, divided
  • ½ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese (60 g)
  • 1 oz (30 g) sharp cheddar cheese, grated
  • 2 oz (55 g) Gouda cheese, grated
  • 2 oz (55 g) Fontina cheese, grated

For the Tomato soup:

  • ⅓ cup olive oil (75 g)
  • 3 (12 oz) pkgs cherry tomatoes (340 g)
  • 1 small yellow onion, cut into 1 inch wedges (150 g)
  • 1 garlic clove, smashed
  • Kosher salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Sugar, as needed
  • ¼ cup heavy cream (60 ml)
  • Parsley, for garnish

Steps

For the Grilled Cheese

  1. On one side of each piece of bread, spread the mayonnaise and 1 tablespoon of butter over the surface, all the way to the edges. Sprinkle ¼ cup of Parmesan evenly over the mayonnaise, and lightly press into the surface of the bread. Flip each piece over and add the cheddar, Gouda and fontina cheese to one piece of bread. Top with the second piece and set aside.
  2. In a medium nonstick skillet over medium heat, add 1 tablespoon of butter. Once the butter is lightly sizzling, add the grilled cheese. Cook for 3-4 minutes, or until one side is deeply golden brown, then flip and cook for another 3-4 minutes.
  3. In the last 1-2 minutes of cooking, cover the skillet with a lid to allow the grilled cheese to lightly steam and melt any cheese that has not yet melted.
  4. Remove the grilled cheese from the pan and turn the heat to low. Add the remaining Parmesan cheese directly onto the skillet.
  5. Add the grilled cheese back to the skillet, directly on top of the Parmesan. Cook for 1-2 minutes, or until the Parmesan is crispy and crunchy on the bottom.
  6. Transfer to a cutting board and let the grilled cheese cool for 2-3 minutes. Once it is cool enough to handle, slice it in half and serve it with tomato soup.

For the Tomato Soup

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (218°C).
  2. Drizzle half of the olive oil onto the sheet tray, then add the cherry tomatoes, onion and garlic. Add the rest of the olive oil over top, then season generously with salt and pepper.
  3. Bake for 35-45 minutes, or until the tomatoes have burst and begun to caramelize around the edges.
  4. Remove from the oven and allow to cool for 5 minutes.
  5. Add the tomatoes, onion, and garlic, as well as any juices on the sheet tray to a high powered blender. Blend on medium high for 1 minute, or until fully pureed. Taste and season with additional salt, pepper, and sugar, if needed.
  6. While the tomatoes are blending, bring 2 cups of water to a boil.
  7. If more liquid is needed to thin the consistency of the soup, add half of the hot water to the sheet tray. Scrape up any browned bits with a wooden spoon or spatula. Carefully transfer that to the blender. If more is needed, add water in ¼ cup increments to the blender.
  8. Add the heavy cream and blend again for 20 seconds. If the mixture is too thick, add ¼ cup of water incrementally, or until the desired consistency is achieved.
