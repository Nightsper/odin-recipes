Description

Tiramisu is a dessert that cuts through the clutter—rich, layered, and unpretentious. This recipe strips back the hype and delivers authentic Italian flavors: espresso-soaked ladyfingers, creamy mascarpone, and a dusting of cocoa that ties it all together. It's straightforward, indulgent, and perfect for those who want a dessert that’s both impressive and easy to make. Enjoy every bite without the fuss.

Ingredients

    Base layer:

  • 48 Ladyfingers
  • 2 cups Strong Brewed Coffee
  • Sugar to taste

    • Cream layer:

  • 3 cups Heavy Cream
  • 8 oz Mascarpone Cheese or Cream Cheese (room temperature)
  • ½ cup Sugar

    • Topping layer:

  • dutch processed cocoa powder

Steps

  1. Brew strong coffee, and sugar to taste, stir until the sugar is completely dissolved, and allow to cool.
  2. In a mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream until it forms stiff peaks. Add mascarpone cheese and sugar until smooth.
  3. Gently fold the mascarpone mixture into the whipped cream until well combined.
  4. In a 13" x 9" inch pan quickly soak each of the ladyfingers into the coffee syrup and assemble one layer.
  5. Spread half of the mascarpone cream on top of the ladyfingers creating a second layer. Follow steps 4 - 5 to create two more layers.
  6. Cover the tiramisu with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4-5 hours or better yet overnight to allow the mixture to solidify.
  7. Before serving, dust the top of the tiramisu with unsweetened cocoa powder using a fine sieve.
  8. Slice, serve, and enjoy your homemade Mascarpone Cheese Tiramisu!
