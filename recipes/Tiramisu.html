Tiramisu

<<Back to Home

Description

Tiramisu is a dessert that cuts through the clutter—rich, layered, and unpretentious. This recipe strips back the hype and delivers authentic Italian flavors: espresso-soaked ladyfingers, creamy mascarpone, and a dusting of cocoa that ties it all together. It's straightforward, indulgent, and perfect for those who want a dessert that’s both impressive and easy to make. Enjoy every bite without the fuss.

Ingredients

Base layer:

  • 48 ladyfingers
  • 2 cups strong brewed coffee
  • sugar to taste

Cream layer:

  • 3 cups heavy cream
  • 8 oz Mascarpone Cheese or Cream Cheese (room temperature)
  • ½ cup Sugar

Topping layer:

  • dutch processed cocoa powder

Steps

  1. Brew strong coffee, and add sugar to taste, stir until the sugar is completely dissolved, and allow to cool.
  2. In a cold mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream until it forms stiff peaks.
  3. In a separate bowl add mascarpone cheese (or cream cheese) and sugar and mix until smooth
  4. Gently fold the mascarpone mixture into the whipped cream until well combined.
  5. In a 13" x 9" inch pan quickly soak each of the ladyfingers into the coffee syrup and assemble one layer.
  6. Spread half of the mascarpone cream on top of the ladyfingers creating a second layer. Follow steps 4 - 5 to create two more layers.
  7. Cover the tiramisu with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4-5 hours or better yet overnight to allow the mixture to solidify.
  8. Before serving, dust the top of the tiramisu with unsweetened cocoa powder using a fine sieve.
  9. Slice, serve, and enjoy your homemade Mascarpone Cheese Tiramisu!
